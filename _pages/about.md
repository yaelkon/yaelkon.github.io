I am a second-year PhD student in the [Department of Computer Science](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/) at the **University of Cambridge**, supervised by [Prof. Mateja Jamnik](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~mj201/). My research focuses on **algorithmic bias mitigation** in AI systems using **explainable AI (XAI)** methods — building tools that not only explain model decisions, but leverage those explanations to make AI fairer and more trustworthy.

Before Cambridge, I spent five years as a Research Scientist at SightX AI, designing and deploying real-time deep learning systems for aerial object detection on edge hardware. I hold an MSc with Distinction (GPA 96/100) from Ben-Gurion University, where I developed probabilistic methods for interpreting deep neural networks.

My research interests include **trustworthy & fair AI**, **explainable AI (XAI)**, **representation learning**, and **probabilistic model interpretation**.
